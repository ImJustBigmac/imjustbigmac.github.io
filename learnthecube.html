Beginner's Method Tutorial

The Beginner Method is the easiest way to solve the 3x3 Rubik's Cube. It breaks the solution down into 7 simple steps, layer by layer (LBL).

1.

The White Cross

Form a cross shape on the **White** face, ensuring the **edge pieces** match the center colors of the adjacent sides (e.g., White-Red edge matches the White and Red centers).

2.

Complete the First Layer (F2L)

Insert the four **white corner pieces** to complete the entire first layer. This is done by holding the cube so the White side is on the bottom, and using a simple 4-move algorithm called the **Sledgehammer** (R U R' U') or its reverse.

3.

Complete the Second Layer

Solve the four **middle layer edge pieces** (no yellow color). You'll use two mirror-image algorithms (one for the left side, one for the right side) to move the edge from the top layer into the middle layer.

4.

The Yellow Cross

Ignore the corner pieces for now. Use the **F R U R' U' F'** algorithm one or more times to form a Yellow Cross on the top face. You may start with a dot, a small 'L' shape, or a line.

5.

Orient the Last Layer (OLL)

Flip all the remaining **Yellow corner pieces** so the Yellow face is fully complete. Use the **R U R' U R U2 R'** algorithm (Sune) while holding the cube in specific orientations until the whole top face is yellow.

6.

Position the Last Layer Corners (PLL)

Get the **corner pieces** into their correct *position* (even if they're twisted). Use the **U R U' L' U R' U' L** algorithm until at least two adjacent corners are in the correct spot.

7.

Position the Last Layer Edges

The final step! Use the algorithm to cycle the remaining **edge pieces** until the cube is solved: **R2 U R U R' U' R' U' R' U R'** (or similar 11-move algorithm).

Keep practicing those algorithms! The more you use them, the faster you'll get.
